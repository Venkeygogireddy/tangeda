About Tangeda

Tangeda is a culturally rich village located in Andhra Pradesh, known for its historic roots, vibrant festivals, and close-knit community. Surrounded by fertile lands and temples, Tangeda has preserved its traditional way of life while embracing development in education and services.

History of Tangeda

Tangeda is a village with deep historical roots, believed to be founded centuries ago by the King Anumula Machireddy Garu. Over the years, the village has witnessed cultural transformation, temple developments, and traditional festivals that are still celebrated today.

The royal legacy continues to shape the values and unity of the people in the community.

Village Timeline

  • 1600s: Founding of Tangeda by Anumula Machireddy Garu
  • 1800s: Temples constructed in the village center
  • 1947: Participation in India’s independence movement
  • 1980s: Village school and water system established
  • 2020: Digital initiatives and festival archives began
